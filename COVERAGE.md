# Coverage

## What Is Included

- **Pest and disease profiles**: 48 threats across Dutch arable agriculture, fruit, bolgewassen, and glastuinbouw -- diseases (Phytophthora, Septoria, meeldauw, bruine roest, gele roest, oogvlekkenziekte, halmdoder, Fusarium, Rhizoctonia, ratelziekte, schurft, zilverschurft, droogrot, natrot, vergelingsziekte, cercospora, knolvoet, schurft Venturia, vruchtboomkanker, perevuur, tulpenvuur, Pythium, Fusarium oxysporum, builenbrand) and pests (bladluis, coloradokever, aardappelcysteaaltje, stengelaaltje, trips, bladrandkever, graanhaantje, fritvlieg, bietenvlieg, bietencysteaaltje, preimot, koolvlieg, koolwitje, kooluil, maisstengelborer, fruitmot, appelbloesemkever, bloedluis, wittevlieg, spint, Californische trips, mineervlieg)
- **Symptom database** with confidence levels (diagnostic, suggestive, associated) for differential diagnosis -- 158 symptom records across all 48 pests
- **Treatment options**: chemical, cultural, and biological approaches with active substances, Ctgb registration, timing, resistance risk -- 51 treatment records
- **IPM guidance**: monitoring thresholds (schadedrempels), decision guides, cultural controls -- 21 guidance records covering aardappelen, wintertarwe, granen, suikerbieten, uien, kool, appels, and glastuinbouw
- **Approved products**: 53 Ctgb-registered gewasbeschermingsmiddelen including fungiciden, insecticiden, herbiciden, biologische middelen, nematiciden, and groeiregulatoren -- with W-nummers, active substances, approved crops, and expiry dates

## What Is NOT Included

- **Other EU member state approvals** -- only Dutch Ctgb-registered products are included
- **Detailed resistance data** -- general resistance risk notes are included, not full resistance maps or monitoring data
- **Real-time product approval changes** -- Ctgb updates are ingested periodically, not in real time
- **Spray application rates** -- always refer to product labels for current authorised rates
- **CLM environmental scores** -- planned for future release
- **Weed species** -- herbicide products are listed but weed identification is not covered
- **Ornamental-specific pests** -- glastuinbouw coverage focuses on major food crop pests

## Known Gaps

1. Some crop-pest combinations lack dedicated IPM records (mais, bolgewassen)
2. FTS5 search quality varies with query phrasing -- use specific pest or symptom terms for best results (Dutch or Latin names work)
3. Approved product data depends on Ctgb publication schedule; always verify current approvals before use
4. Quarantine organism regulations (coloradokever, aardappelcysteaaltje, perevuur) reference NVWA requirements but do not replicate full regulatory text
5. Glastuinbouw biological control agents are listed as treatments but not all have Ctgb W-nummers (natural enemies)
